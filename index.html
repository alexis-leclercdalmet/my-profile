Hi there! I'm Alexis

I'm a student in a double degree program : the Master's degree in information-communication at the Panthéon-Assas University and the Global BBA at ESSEC. I am a committed person, especially in favor of human rights and social justice.

Discover my course program !

#1 A young journalist

My path in the youth press began in high school when I joined the school newspaper: Le Charcotidien. Then I went through La Mouette Bâillonnée, newspaper of the Marcelin Berthelot high school (94). At that time, I crossed the path of the national association of promotion and defence of the press of young initiative: Jets d'encre. If I am still a member of the association, I have been an administrator for 2 years. Outcome of the meeting with the members of the association, I founded the young associative webjournal Hexag'Online, of which I was editor in chief! In short, a decade of commitment to the freedom of expression of young people.

#2 A queer activist

I have been fighting every day since high school for the rights of LGBTQ+ people. I dream of a world in which heteropatriarchy, as it has been understood by the humanities and social sciences, is no more. I was a member of the French association of LGBTQI+ journalists and have just joined Assas LGBT+. If I am already involved in protests, in the accompaniment of the concerned people and in the pedagogy with the non-concerned ones, I wish to continue this commitment through lobbying and politics.

#3 An aspiring politician

When I was only 10 years old, I was already a municipal councillor for children and young people in my town, Joinville-le-Pont (94). In high school, I joined the student life council and was the founding president of the "Maison des Lycéens", an association run by students for students. When I finally arrived at the university, I was vice-president and founder of the association of my degree, degree representative and elected to the council of the French Press Institute. Today, I am elected to the academic council of the University of Paris II Panthéon-Assas for Assas In Progress, a federation of 7 associations committed to more humanism, solidarity and social justice at the university. I believe that the values I defend on a daily basis must be carried into the decision-making spheres of our societies, and that this happens through a political commitment.
